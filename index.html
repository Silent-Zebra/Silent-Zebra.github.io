About Me

I am a PhD student supervised by Roger Grosse at the University of Toronto. My research goal is to help guide the development of artificial intelligence in a way that benefits the greater good, with a focus on AI alignment.

Research

My most recent paper is on applying twisted Sequential Monte Carlo for controlled generation from language models. This work aims to provide new methods for aligning language models and evaluating their behavior.

I completed my MSc in September 2022, working on more consistently learning reciprocity-based cooperation in social dilemmas.

For all my publications, see my Google Scholar.

My Journey

I started my academic journey in Finance and Economics (2011-2015). After working in finance and exploring various interests including competitive gaming and game development, I became fascinated by AI developments. This led me to return to university for computer science studies in 2018, with the goal of conducting AI alignment research.

My diverse background in finance and computer science has given me a unique perspective on AI research and its potential impact on society.
